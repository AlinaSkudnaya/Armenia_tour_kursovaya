What awaits you?

Yerevan at a glance

You will go up to the observation deck of the architectural and monumental complex "Cascade" and look at the Armenian capital from above. I will tell you how and why the "Cascade" was built, what role it plays in the modern life of the city and what legends it conceals in its bas-reliefs. Next, you will see a monument to the architect Tamanyan and imagine how he saw Yerevan 100 years ago. And I will also tell you about another great inhabitant of the capital - the composer Komitas and his tragic fate.

A city with the aroma of coffee and wine

We will walk along the old streets of Yerevan, where it is always noisy and fun. Over a cup of aromatic coffee, I will share the peculiarities of our traditions and mentality, I will try to confirm or refute popular stereotypes about Armenians and Armenia. And lovers of the secrets of the past and the secrets of the future will be able to tell fortunes on the coffee grounds! Next, I will take you along the “wine” street of Yerevan, tell you about local winemaking and show you what places you can go to in the evening.

The Main Town Square

In the silence and coolness of the ancient Zoravor Church, I will introduce you to the history of Christianity in the country. You will hear about the similarities and differences between the Armenian Gregorian and Russian Orthodox churches. And also why the local church was of great importance even during the Soviet Union. The culmination of the walk will be the central square of the city - it is here that the history of the country has been made for centuries, and residents and guests of the city relax and admire the singing fountains on weekdays.
